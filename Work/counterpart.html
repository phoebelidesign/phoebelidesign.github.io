Counterpart

newsprint, ink

Entering the world as an identical twin, I have always found the dual nature of identity to be an intriguing subject to ponder. ‘Counterpart’ explores the notion of existing as a constituent of a whole, reflecting on the lived experience of twinhood. It contemplates the delicate balance between symmetry and duality, where the self is simultaneously distinct and deeply linked to another.

Acknowledging the complexities of this existence, I consider how this dynamic intertwines with a broader narrative - one in which twin identity is often misunderstood, oversimplified, or commodified by the wider world.

The chosen format, a newspaper, serves as a vessel that mirrors the accessibility and misinterpretation of twin identity. Through this medium, ‘Counterpart’ captures the inherent tension of being part of a larger whole, delving into the exploration of an identity that exists simultaneously revealed and concealed.
